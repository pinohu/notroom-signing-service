## 1. NOTARY PUBLIC COMMISSION (REQUIRED - State of PA)

### Status: ❌ INCOMPLETE - MUST COMPLETE BEFORE LAUNCH

**Issuing Authority:** PA Department of State, Bureau of Commissions, Elections, and Legislation

**Required Steps:**
- [ ] Complete mandatory 3-hour pre-commission training course (within 6 months of application)
  - Approved providers: PA Association of Notaries, National Notary Association
  - Cost: ~$50-150
  
- [ ] Pass Pennsylvania Notary Exam via Pearson VUE
  - Schedule at pearsonvue.com/pa/notary
  - Fee: Included in application
  - Study Guide: PA RULONA (57 Pa.C.S. § 301 et seq.)

- [ ] Submit Online Application to PA Department of State
  - Website: dos.pa.gov
  - Fee: $42 (4-year commission term)
  - Processing: 2-4 weeks

- [ ] Obtain $10,000 Surety Bond (4-year term)
  - Bond companies: National Notary Association, SuretyBonds.com
  - Cost: ~$50-100 for 4 years
  - Bond must cover entire commission period

- [ ] File Bond, Take Oath & Record with County Recorder of Deeds
  - **DEADLINE: Within 45 days of appointment or commission is nullified**
  - Location: Erie County Recorder of Deeds Office
  - Requirements: Bring appointment letter, original bond, photo ID
  - Record: Bond, oath of office, official signature
  - Fee: County recording fee (varies, ~$25-50)

- [ ] Purchase Official Notary Seal (Rubber Stamp)
  - Requirements per PA law:
    * Your name
    * "Notary Public"
    * "Commonwealth of Pennsylvania"
    * County name
    * Commission expiration date
  - Optional: Embosser (NOT substitute for ink seal)
  - Suppliers: ProStamps.com, NNA, Pennsylvania suppliers

- [ ] Purchase/Create Notary Journal
  - **REQUIRED by PA law** - must keep chronological record
  - Options: Paper (bound, tamper-evident) OR electronic (secure, tamper-evident)
  - Must record: Date/time, type of act, document type, signer info, ID method, fee charged
  - Retention: Keep for duration of commission + recommended 10 years

**Website References:**
- Application: dos.pa.gov/VotingElections/OtherServicesEvents/Pages/Notaries-Public.aspx
- Laws: 57 Pa.C.S. § 301 et seq. (RULONA)
- Regulations: 4 Pa. Code Chapter 161

**Cost Summary:** ~$242-292 total
- Application: $42
- Training: $50-150
- Bond: $50-100
- Seal & Journal: $50-100
- Recording Fee: ~$25-50

## 2. REMOTE ONLINE NOTARIZATION (RON) AUTHORIZATION

### Status: ❌ INCOMPLETE - REQUIRED FOR RON SERVICES

**Issuing Authority:** PA Department of State

**Legal Basis:** Act 97 of 2020 (permanent RON authorization, effective Oct 29, 2020)

**Required Steps:**
- [ ] **PREREQUISITE:** Must have active PA notary commission first

- [ ] Submit Electronic/Remote Notarization Application to PA DOS
  - Online form at DOS website
  - **NO ADDITIONAL FEE** beyond notary commission
  - Specify RON technology platform(s) you will use

- [ ] Select PA-Approved RON Technology Platform
  - **REQUIRED:** Platform must be on PA DOS approved provider list
  - Popular options: Notarize, DocuSign, DocVerify, Pavaso, SIGNiX, NotaryCam
  - Platform must provide:
    * Real-time audio-video communication
    * Tamper-evident technology
    * Multi-factor identity proofing (credential analysis + KBA)
    * 10-year secure recording storage
  - Platform fees: Varies ($20-50/session or subscription model)

- [ ] Obtain Digital Certificate for Electronic Signature
  - Usually provided by RON platform
  - Cost: May be included in platform fee

- [ ] Obtain Electronic Notary Seal
  - Usually provided by RON platform
  - Must meet PA standards (same info as physical seal)

- [ ] Verify Compliance Requirements
  - [ ] Must be physically located in PA during RON sessions
  - [ ] Can notarize for signers located anywhere (including outside U.S.)
  - [ ] Must verify ID via platform's credential analysis + KBA OR personal knowledge
  - [ ] Must record entire audio-video session
  - [ ] Must store recordings for 10 years minimum
  - [ ] Must note in journal: "Remote via audio-video; ID verified by [method]"

- [ ] **UPDATE WEBSITE:** Add specific RON platform name(s) to disclaimers
  - Example: "I use Notarize.com, a PA Department of State-approved RON platform"

**Website References:**
- RON FAQ: dos.pa.gov (search "remote online notarization")
- Approved Providers: PA DOS maintains updated list
- Law: 57 Pa.C.S. § 306 (RON provisions)

**Cost Summary:** ~$200-500/year
- DOS Authorization: $0
- RON Platform: $200-500/year (varies by provider and volume)

## 3. LOAN SIGNING AGENT CERTIFICATION (INDUSTRY STANDARD)

### Status: ⚠️  RECOMMENDED - NOT STATE-REQUIRED BUT INDUSTRY-EXPECTED

**Note:** PA does not require separate state licensing for signing agents, but title companies/lenders require industry credentials.

**Required Steps:**
- [ ] Complete Loan Signing Agent Training Course
  - Providers: National Notary Association (NNA), Loan Signing System, Notary2Pro
  - Cost: $99-299
  - Content: Loan document types, signing procedures, MISMO standards

- [ ] Pass Signing Agent Certification Exam
  - NNA Certified Signing Agent credential recommended
  - Exam covers: Loan documents, notary law, signing procedures
  - Renewal: Annual (requires background check)

- [ ] Obtain Enhanced Background Check
  - **REQUIRED by most title companies** - annually
  - Must cover: Criminal history, credit check, fraud screening
  - Providers: NNA, background check companies
  - Cost: $40-70/year

- [ ] Obtain Higher E&O Insurance
  - **State Requirement:** None (only $10K bond for notary commission)
  - **Industry Requirement:** $25,000 - $100,000 E&O insurance
  - **Your Website Claims:** $100K coverage
  - Providers: Notary insurance carriers
  - Cost: $150-400/year for $100K coverage

- [ ] Obtain EIN (if operating as business)
  - IRS.gov - free online application
  - Use for 1099 forms instead of SSN

- [ ] **UPDATE WEBSITE:** Confirm you actually have these credentials
  - "NNA certified" - must have active NNA certification
  - "$100K E&O insurance" - must have policy in force

**Cost Summary:** ~$289-769 first year, ~$190-470/year ongoing
- Training: $99-299 (one-time)
- Certification Exam: Included in training
- Background Check: $40-70 (annual)
- E&O Insurance: $150-400 (annual)

## 6. COMMERCIAL REGISTERED OFFICE PROVIDER (CROP) REGISTRATION

### Status: ❌ CRITICAL - MUST REGISTER BEFORE OFFERING CROP SERVICES

**Issuing Authority:** PA Department of State, Bureau of Corporations and Charitable Organizations

**Legal Basis:** 15 Pa.C.S. § 109, § 415 (Model Registered Agents Act)

**REQUIRED Before Offering Registered Office Services:**

- [ ] **REGISTER AS CROP with PA Department of State**
  - Submit CROP Registration Statement to:
    Bureau of Corporations and Charitable Organizations
    PA Department of State
    301 North Office Building
    Harrisburg, PA 17120
  
  - Registration must include:
    * Your name/business name
    * Business type and address
    * Statement that you are in business as CROP
    * Contact information
  
  - Fee: Minimal or none (confirm with Bureau)
  - Processing: Usually approved quickly
  
  - **Result:** Your name added to official PA DOS CROP list

- [ ] Maintain Physical PA Address (No P.O. Box alone)
  - Must be staffed/monitored during business hours
  - Must accept service of process, certified mail, official notices

- [ ] Create CROP Service Contracts
  - Each client company must have written agreement
  - PA law prohibits listing CROP without their consent
  - Contract should specify: Services, fees, term, mail forwarding, etc.

- [ ] **UPDATE WEBSITE AFTER REGISTRATION:**
  - Change from "PA-compliant CROP" to:
    "Registered Commercial Registered Office Provider with PA Department of State Bureau of Corporations"
  - Add CROP registration date or reference number if provided

**Critical UPL Compliance:**

- [x] Website correctly states: "I do not draft custom legal documents"
